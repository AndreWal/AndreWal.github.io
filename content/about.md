I'm a research assistant at the [Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen](https://www.unisg.ch/en/universitaet/schools/economics-and-political-science/ueber-seps/politikwissenschaftliches-departement). My areas of interest are comparative political economy, comparative politics and statistical methods.

In my PhD project, I investigate the effect of electoral competition, interstate cooperation and the interaction of direct democracy and coalition governments on social policies and social expenditure. In addition, I have been involved in data collections about the impact of colonialism in Africa and Asia and the legislative and executive composition in the Swiss Cantons. Moreover, I work with [Patrick Emmenegger](https://pemmenegger.com/) on the introduction of proportional representation and income taxation in Switzerland.  Methodological, I'm interested in Econometrics, GIS and statistical programming in R.

As a teaching assistant, I have supported many courses on research designs, qualitative and quantitative methods. For more information, feel free to browse my website.
