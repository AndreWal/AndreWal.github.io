I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the [Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen](https://www.unisg.ch/en/universitaet/schools/economics-and-political-science/ueber-seps/politikwissenschaftliches-departement). My areas of interest are comparative political economy, comparative politics and statistical methods. More specifically, I'm interested in the formation and development of parties and electoral institutions as well as social and economic policies in the 19th and 20th century. In addition, I focus on current topics on comparative political economy. My work has been published in the Journal of Politics, the Journal of European Social Policy and the Journal of World-Systems Research. Methodologically, I'm interested in Econometrics, GIS and statistical programming in R. For a closer look, [see my publications](/publications/) or [research](/research/).

As a teaching assistant, I have supported many courses on research designs, qualitative and quantitative methods. Starting in spring 2018, I'm also teaching different subjects in comparative politics and comparative political economy [(past and current teaching)](/teaching/).
