# Biography

I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the [Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen](https://www.unisg.ch/en/universitaet/schools/economics-and-political-science/ueber-seps/politikwissenschaftliches-departement). In addition, I was a visiting scholar at the [Research Center on Inequality and Social Policy at the University of Bremen](http://www.socium.uni-bremen.de) (Oktober 2018 - March 2019) and will be visiting the [Department of Politics and Public Administration at the University of Konstanz](https://www.polver.uni-konstanz.de/en/) (September 2019 - February 2020). I work at the intersection of comparative political economy and comparative politics. More specifically, I'm interested in electoral systems choice, the determinants of districting and gerrymandering as well as how electoral systems affect the success of radical parties. My work in comparative political economy centers around the question of how political institutions shape welfare state development and the emergence of the tax state. Furthermore, I use GIS data to investigate the origins of pre-modern states. My work has been published in the Journal of Politics, the Journal of European Social Policy, the Swiss Political Science Review and Electoral Studies. For a closer look, [see my publications](#publications), [research](#research) or my {{% staticref "files/New_CV.pdf" %}}Curriculum Vitae{{% /staticref %}}.

I have supported many courses on research designs, qualitative and quantitative methods over the years. I currently teach introductory courses on electoral systems, statistics, and QCA [(past and current teaching)](#teaching).
