# Biography

I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the [Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen](https://www.unisg.ch/en/universitaet/schools/economics-and-political-science/ueber-seps/politikwissenschaftliches-departement) and visiting scholar at the [Research Center on Inequality and Social Policy](http://www.socium.uni-bremen.de) (Oktober 2018 - March 2019) , working at the intersection of comparative political economy and comparative politics. More specifically, I'm interested in the formation and development of parties and electoral institutions as well as social and economic policies in the 19th and early 20th century. In addition, I focus on current topics on comparative political economy. My work has been published in the Journal of Politics, the Journal of European Social Policy and the Journal of World-Systems Research. Methodologically, I'm interested in Econometrics, GIS and statistical programming in R. For a closer look, [see my publications](#publications) or [research](#research).

I have supported many courses on research designs, qualitative and quantitative methods over the years. I currently teach introductory courses on electoral systems and statistics [(past and current teaching)](#teaching).
