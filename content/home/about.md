# Biography

I'm a postdoctoral researcher at the [Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen](https://www.unisg.ch/en/universitaet/schools/economics-and-political-science/ueber-seps/politikwissenschaftliches-departement). Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute of Political Studies (University of Lausanne), a visiting scholar at the [Research Center on Inequality and Social Policy](http://www.socium.uni-bremen.de) (university of Bremen), and the [Department of Politics and Public Administration](https://www.polver.uni-konstanz.de/en/) (University of Konstanz). I work at the intersection of comparative political economy and comparative politics. More specifically, I'm interested in electoral systems choice, the determinants of districting and gerrymandering, and how electoral systems affect the success of radical parties. My work in comparative political economy centers around the question of how political institutions shape welfare state development and the emergence of the tax state. Furthermore, I use GIS data to investigate the origins of pre-modern states and the impact of climate change on party programs and support for environmental policies. My work has been published at the American Political Science Review, the Journal of Politics, Socio-Economic Review, the Journal of European Social Policy, and Electoral Studies. For a closer look, [see my publications](#publications) or [research](#research). My CV (which is probably more up to date than the website) can be found here: [CV](https://www.dropbox.com/s/ft4hdlfchlpa95w/New_CV_engl.pdf?dl=0).

I have supported many courses on research designs, qualitative and quantitative methods over the years. I currently teach introductory courses on electoral systems and statistics [(past and current teaching)](#teaching).
