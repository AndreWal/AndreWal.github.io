Comparative Politics

HSG International Fellowship

André Walter

Tags: Swiss Politics; History; Comparative Politics

I’ve got news that I have been awared with the International Postdoctoral Fellowship! This great opportunity allows me to conduct independent research for up to three years on a number of topics of my choosing at the University of St. Gallen. I planning to focus on two projects:

  • The introduction of proportional representation

I haven written a piece with Patrick Emmenegger on the introduction of PR for the election of the Swiss National Council (currently under review). In a subsequent paper, we are investigating the adoption of PR in the Swiss cantons. I’m going to present the first draft in February 2018 at the Swiss Political Science Annual Conference in Geneva. After that, we want to look at the introduction of PR in other European countries.

  • Party formation and electoral competition

In the second project, I focus on the formation of new parties such as catholic and social democratic parties and how established parties reacted to the emergence of these competitors. While catholic and social democratic parties came to dominate the party systems in all democratic states in Europe during the 20th century, we know only little about their emergence. Therefore, I’m going to investigate a number of questions related to their development. For instance, why did catholic parties forge cross-class alliances even though they espoused anti-socialist and conservative positions? Did established parties increasingly form electoral alliances to contain the socialist threat? Why do more than two parties compete in single member districts?

For both projects, I have started with the collection of electoral district data for the German Reich as well as the Swiss cantons. To get things done, I will hire a student assistant to support me in the next months. Stay tuned for the job ad.

About the Author

André Walter is a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen.
