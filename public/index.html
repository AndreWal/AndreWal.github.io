Biography

I’m a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen, working at the intersection of comparative political economy and comparative politics. More specifically, I’m interested in the formation and development of parties and electoral institutions as well as social and economic policies in the 19th and 20th century. In addition, I focus on current topics on comparative political economy. My work has been published in the Journal of Politics, the Journal of European Social Policy and the Journal of World-Systems Research. Methodologically, I’m interested in Econometrics, GIS and statistical programming in R. For a closer look, see my publications or research.

I have supported many courses on research designs, qualitative and quantitative methods over the years. I currently teach introductory courses to electoral systems and statistics (past and current teaching).

New Publication Forthcoming in JoESP

My article, “Taking the Initiative. Direct Democracy, Coalition Governments, and Welfare State Expansion”, has been accepted at the Journal of Eruopean Social Policy. Below is the abstract: Direct democratic rights are increasingly adopted around the world. However, what are the consequences of the introduction of initiative and referendum rights for welfare state development? The existing literature holds a pessimistic view on the role of direct democracy for welfare state development and generosity.

New Publication Forthcoming in JoP

Another article, “A Race to the Middle. The Politics of Interstate Cost Distribution and Welfare State Expansion”, has been accepted at the Journal of Politics. Below is the abstract: Previous research on welfare state development in federal states has claimed that interstate competition induces a race to the bottom with regard to social expenditure. While existing research has focused on the impact of migration inflows of poor households on states with high provision of social expenditure, it has remained silent with regard to migration outflows.
