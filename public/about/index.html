About

I’m a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen. My areas of interest are comparative political economy, comparative politics and statistical methods. More specifically, I’m interested in the formation and development of parties and electoral institutions as well as social and economic policies in the 19th and early 20th century. In addition, I focus on current topics on comparative political economy. Methodological, I’m interested in Econometrics, GIS and statistical programming in R. For a closer look, see my publications or research.

As a teaching assistant, I have supported many courses on research designs, qualitative and quantitative methods. Starting in spring 2018, I’m going to expand my teaching repertoire to different subjects in comparative politics (past and current teaching).
