About

I’m a research assistant at the Department of Political Science, University of St. Gallen. My areas of interest are comparative political economy, comparative politics and statistical methods. More specifically, I’m interested in the formation and development of parties and electoral institutions as well as social and economic policies in the 19th and early 20th century. In addition, I focus on current topics on comparative political economy. For a closer look, see my publications.

In my PhD project, I have investigated the effect of electoral competition, interstate cooperation and the interaction of direct democracy and coalition governments on social policies and social expenditure. In addition, I have been involved in data collections about the impact of colonialism in Africa and Asia and the legislative and executive composition in the Swiss Cantons. Moreover, I work with Patrick Emmenegger on the introduction of proportional representation and income taxation in Switzerland. Methodological, I’m interested in Econometrics, GIS and statistical programming in R.

As a teaching assistant, I have supported many courses on research designs, qualitative and quantitative methods (past and current teaching).
